Hampton Roads LGAC Concerns 

The purpose of the LGAC is to advise the Executive Council on how to most 

effectively, equitably, and expeditiously implement the projects required to 

engage the support of local governments to achieve the goals of the Bay 

Agreement.  

  

LGAC’s goal is to develop and execute strategy, to ensure continued local 

government participation, and input in the design, development, and 

implementation of programs to protect and improve the Chesapeake Bay.

 

Agenda Item #7

 Did not address specific concerns like the following examples: 



Virginia needs to demonstrate to localities that the data they have submitted 
has been incorporated into the 2012 Progress Run. State agencies should 
develop a transparent and structured process to collect data for future 
progress runs.  

   



The new program structure will divide nonpoint source strategies between 
two agencies and could exacerbate the existing problems. The state should 
create a multi-agency team to ensure state representatives to the Bay 
Program have full access and knowledge of existing data, past decisions about 
data submittals and modeling assumptions. 

   

 Offered to have staff meet with LGAC members.
